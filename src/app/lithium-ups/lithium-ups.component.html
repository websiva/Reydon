About the Product

UPS/Inverter system supplies conditioned utility power to its outlets and keeps its internal battery charged as long as the electricity is on. It also safeguards your data and valuable gadgets against power issues such as power surges and unexpected voltages. If the power goes out, the UPS/Inverter system provides backup power from its internal battery. This allows your devices to stay on during a power outage, which is especially useful for devices like computers that can lose data when they turn off unexpectedly.

Your appliances are under constant threat from power problems. Even a brief loss of power or a surge can cause priceless data to be corrupted, ranging from corporate documents to family images. This can be avoided with the use of a UPS. UPS systems are simple to operate. Simply connect the UPS to a wall outlet, connect your equipment to the UPS outlets, and turn the UPS on. Extra functions, such as interacting with a computer or modifying the default settings, are available on most UPS systems, but they are not required.

The UPS/Inverter battery is the integral component of industrial computers and other usages. During abrupt power outages, it acts as a reliable shield to defend your valuable devices.
